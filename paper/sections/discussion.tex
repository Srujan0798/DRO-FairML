\subsection{The Feature-Demography Correlation Hypothesis}
Our central hypothesis is that \textbf{DRO-FAIR requires features to naturally encode demographic information} to be effective under corruption.

\textbf{Tabular data:} Features like income and credit score naturally correlate with protected attributes. Even with corrupted labels, DRO identifies robust patterns.

\textbf{Image features (ResNet18):} Trained on ImageNet, ResNet18 extracts visual features that are \textit{demographic-agnostic}. Under label corruption, DRO has no robust signal---its worst-case reweighting amplifies the spurious fairness signal from corrupted labels.

\subsection{Mechanism}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Corrupted labels introduce a spurious fairness constraint
\item DRO's Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ increases to enforce it
\item Without feature-demography correlation, $\lambda$ over-corrects
\item Result: DRO achieves worse fairness than naive training
\end{enumerate}

This reveals a fundamental requirement for DRO: feature-demography correlation. Without it, worst-case reweighting amplifies noise.
